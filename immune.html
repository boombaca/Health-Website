What is Immunity?

Immunity refers to the ability of the immune system, the body’s biological structures, processes and organs that are specially developed for fighting off infections and hence preventing illnesses.

The purpose of the immune system is to protect the body against infection. It helps to identify and destroy the millions of microbes (i.e. bacteria, viruses, parasites, fungi) that penetrate our bodies every day. Strengthening the immune system is perhaps the most important step in achieving resistance to diseases.

Better Sleep Better Immunity

When we sleep, our bodies produce a protein called cytokines, which target infection and inflammation, creating an immune response. Our bodies also produce T-cells during sleep, which are white blood cells that play a critical role in our body’s immune response to an infectious disease such as COVID-19.

16 Foods that Boost Immune System in Children

1. Leafy Vegetables

Vegetables such as cabbage, cauliflower, spinach, broccoli, parsley, kale, lettuce and especially green leafy green veg help in preventing and fighting against infections. Leafy vegetables are loaded with nutrients and micro nutrients such as Vitamins A,C & K, calcium, iron, magnesium, potassium etc. They are also rich in antioxidants like beta carotene and other carotenoids which are essential for a healthy immune system.



2.Root Vegetables

According to Chinese medicine, root vegetables like sweet potatoes, carrots, beetroots, yam, and potatoes are immune strengthening and work specific to the respiratory system. As they are grown underground they absorb the wide amount of nutrients from the soil. Root vegetables are particularly rich in soluble and insoluble fibre. This helps to build the gut health. Root vegetables are considerably rich in vitamins, minerals and fiber which helps in building the immune system.

3. Garlic, Onions, and Ginger

The sulphur compounds present in onion and garlic are said to prevent cancer. The active compounds -flavanoids in all three are immune boosting. The active compounds are Anicillin in garlic, Quercetin in onions. shagaols and gingerols in ginger. Adding them liberally while preparing foods can prevent seasonal illness. It’s always best to make fresh pastes out of garlic and ginger instead of opting for store bought pastes. Garlic, onion and ginger aid digestion and keep gastritis at bay and also enhances heart health.

4. Cayenne Peppers

Cayenne peppers contain the chemical capsaicin, a rich source of vitamin C that aids your immune system in fighting colds and flu. Just one tablespoon of cayenne pepper contains 44% of the RDI Vitamin A, a nutrient that’s crucial in boosting immunity and fighting infection.

5. Squash or Pumpkin

Vegetables that belong to the squash or pumpkin family are loaded with antioxidants, Vitamin A, Vitamin C, manganese, magnesium and folate, all of which strengthen the immune system. Pumpkins alone contain a whopping 245% of the daily requirement of Vitamin A.

6. Apricots

Apricots are known for their anti-inflammatory properties. The anti-oxidants present in apricots help in enhancing the immune system and helps the body resist common diseases. They are packed with Vitamin A, Vitamin C, potassium and lots of dietary fiber.

7. Citrus Fruits

It is a well known fact that citrus fruits like oranges, limes and guavas have a good amount of Vitamin C which helps boost immunity. and antioxidants. Besides this, citrus fruits help the body to absorb the nutrients like iron and support the immune system to grow strong

8. Berries

Any list of super foods is incomplete without berries, and they’re heroes when it comes to boosting immunity. Berries include strawberries, blackberries, raspberries, blueberries and cranberries. Berries are rich in antioxidants like phytochemicals and flavonoids, which help boost the immune system and fight inflammation as well.

9. Pulses

Pulses and lentils are rich in protein, fiber, folate, iron, calcium, magnesium, zinc and potassium. They are one of the easiest ways to get protein, which is essential for all body systems including the immune system. Lentils also contain phytochemicals, plant compounds which help fight against chronic illnesses.

10. Green Peas

Green peas are rich in heart healthy nutrients like Vitamins A, B1, B6 and C. They are also rich in antioxidants like flavonoids, carotenoids, phenolic acids, and polyphenols which help in improving immunity. Besides, they are also an excellent source of protein, with 8 g of protein in one cup of peas.

11. Sprouts

While all food groups have their own set of health benefits, sprouting them increases these benefits manifold. Not just that, sprouting makes the hidden nutrients more bio-available, which means they are made easier to absorb by the body. 12. Nuts and Seeds

Commonly available nuts include almonds, walnuts, pistachios, cashews, peanuts and hazelnuts, while seeds include pumpkin seeds, flax seeds, chia seeds, hemp seeds and sesame seeds. They are a very good source of protein, healthy fats, fibers, vitamins and minerals. Nuts and seeds are rich in Vitamin E which is an important antioxidant and immune booster. 13. Curd/Yogurt

Both curd and yogurt contain loads of healthy bacteria which helps in maintaining a healthy gut. Curd has ‘good bacteria’ or probiotics which maintain the gut ecosystem by keeping illnesses at bay. Recent studies have found that curd and yogurt are packed with immuno-stimulatory effects which enhance the body’s immune response.

14. Turmeric

Turmeric is one of the most effective medicine cum nutritional supplement available in your kitchen. It’s a wonder spice which has been used for generations to improve health. Turmeric contains curcumin, which provides it with antimicrobial, antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. 15. Honey

Honey is known for its antioxidant and antibacterial properties which makes it a one stop solution for many health woes, including low immunity. Honey helps in improving gut health, and the antioxidants present in honey help in removing the free radicals from the body which cause serious illnesses. Note: Honey should not be given to babies under one year of age.
